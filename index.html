Services & Home Care Types

Understanding Your Home Care Options

Home care comes in many forms. Here's what's available in Pennsylvania and what to expect.

Home Health Care (Skilled)

  • Skilled nursing: wound care, IV therapy, medication management
  • Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy
  • Medical social work
  • Typically covered by Medicare, Medicaid, or insurance
$150 – $250/visit (often $0 with Medicare)

Personal Care / Home Care (Non-Medical)

  • Bathing, dressing, and grooming assistance
  • Meal preparation and light housekeeping
  • Companionship and transportation
  • Medication reminders
$25 – $35/hr ($4,000 – $5,600/mo at 40hr/wk)

Specialized Care

  • Alzheimer's and dementia care
  • Post-surgical recovery
  • Chronic disease management (COPD, CHF, diabetes)
  • Palliative and hospice care
  • Pediatric home health
$30 – $45/hr for specialized caregivers

Getting Started

Tips for Patients & Family Caregivers

Whether you're exploring options for yourself or a loved one, these tips will help you navigate the process.

For Patients

  1. Talk to your doctor — get a home health referral if you need skilled care
  2. Understand your insurance — Medicare covers skilled home health if you're homebound
  3. Know your rights — you can choose any Medicare-certified agency
  4. Ask about caregiver qualifications and background checks
  5. Start with a needs assessment — most agencies offer free evaluations

For Family Caregivers

  1. Don't wait until crisis — early planning gives you better options
  2. Create a care plan — list daily activities that need support
  3. Interview multiple agencies — ask about caregiver turnover, training, and emergency protocols
  4. Check state licensing — verify the agency is licensed by the PA Department of Health
  5. Look for signs of quality — ask about satisfaction surveys and complaint history
  6. Explore financial assistance — PA has Medicaid waiver programs (OBRA, Aging Waiver, COMMCARE)
Be Prepared

Questions to Ask a Home Care Agency

Print this checklist or keep it handy when interviewing agencies. Good agencies will welcome these questions.

  • Are you licensed by the PA Department of Health?
  • Are caregivers bonded, insured, and background-checked?
  • What is your caregiver-to-client ratio?
  • How do you handle emergencies or schedule changes?
  • Can I meet my caregiver before services begin?
  • What is your process for creating a care plan?
  • Do you accept my insurance or Medicaid waiver?
